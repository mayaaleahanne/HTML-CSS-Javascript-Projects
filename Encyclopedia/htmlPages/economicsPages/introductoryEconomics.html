Introductory Economics

What is microeconomics?

Microeconomics is the study of how individuals and firms make decisions in a world of scarcity (what drives microeconmics). Microeconomics is a series of constrained optimization exercises, where economics agents (firms of indivduals) try to make themselves as well of as possible given their constraints.

Tradeoffs

Opportunity cost: Every action (or inaction) has a cost. Everything done has a next best alternative of what could've been done instead. This is why economics is called the "dismal science". Nothing is free.

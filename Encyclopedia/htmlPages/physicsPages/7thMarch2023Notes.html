Ionizing Radiation

X-Rays

  • What are X-Rays: X-rays are photons of high energy, short wave length radiant energy.
  • How are X-rays produced: X-rays can be produced when a beam of electrons strikes a piece of metal.

Detecting Inonizing Radiation

  • Film badge: Ionizing radiation causes chemical changes in photographic film.
  • Geiger counter: an electrical signal is produced each time an Ionizing particle passes through the counter.
  • Cloud chamber: visible trails are left by ionizing radiation.

Radiation Exposure

Exposure to ionizing radiation depends on:

  • number of ionizing particles
  • time exposed to the particles
  • distance from the radiation source
  • energy of the particles

Average Radiation Exposure

  • The average American's annual exposure is 620 milliriens (mrem)
  • About one half comes from natural background radiation, mostly from radon gas in the air
  • X-rays vary from 0.5 mrem for a dental X-ray to 1,275 mrem for a full-body CT scan. The average is about 50 mrems per X-ray

Radon gas

  • Radon is a radioactive gas that occurs in bedrock, particularly shale.
  • Radon cn seep into basements througgh cracks in the basement walls and floor.
  • A radioactive daughter is the product of decay of a radioactive element. Radon-222 is a decay product (a daughter) of Uranium-238.
  • Radon-222 decays by emitting an alpha particle.

Physics notes

Types of nuclear forces:

  • The weak nuclear force adjusts the relative numbers of neutrons and protons to produce stable nuclei.
  • The strong nuclear force holds protons and neutrons together in the atomic nuclei.
  • The strong nuclear force overcomes the repulsive electromagnetic force amond positive protons.

The weak nuclear force causes decay (things falling apart) while the strong nuclear force holds things together

  • Ionizing radiation strips electrons from atoms, turning the atoms into charged ions.
  • Radioactive nuclei are unstable. They emit ionizing radiation to become more stable.
  • Ionizing radiation is dangerous and bad! It's strong enough to strip away electrons
  • Alpha particles are hellium nuclei (2 neutrons and 2 protons).
  • Beta particles are negative electrons or positive antielectrons.
  • Gammas are very short wavelength radiant energy.
  • Alpha particles the most common
  • Large unstable nuclei have 83 or more protons.
  • Large nuclei can become more stable by reducing the nucleus size.
  • Gamma doesn't change mass
  • Alcohol vapor in the cloud chamber is supercooled by dry ice (frozen carbon dioxide).
  • Radioactive souces emit ionizing radiation that travels through the alcohol vapor.

Conservation of charge and nucleons

  • conservation of charge: The number of positive and negative charges (Z) on each side of the reaction must be equal.
  • Conservation of nucleon number: The number of nucleons (protons and neutrons, A) on each side of the reaction must be equal.
